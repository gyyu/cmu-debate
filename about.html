About Us

Learn about what we believe in and who we are!

Our Manifesto

Carnegie Mellon University Debate is an organization dedicated to enabling every Carnegie Mellon student to communicate masterfully, polish their ability to think critically and analytically, enhance their research skills, and become comfortable with challenging the status quo. We host public speaking workshops and in-house skirmishes to prepare students to succeed in regional and national parliamentary debate tournaments. The issues we debate are diverse and span topics crucial to the engaged member of society, which include but are not limited to: economics, foreign policy, race relations, law, environmentalism, feminism, technology, history, popular culture, and art. What do we do?

Here are the activities we host:

  • Public speaking workshops.
  • Pracice debates.
  • In-house tournaments.
  • Traveling to regional and national tournaments around the nation.
  • Host a national tournament here on campus!

Who can join?

Anybody can join! We welcome people of all backgrounds, ages, and prior public speaking experience. We don't believe in tryouts (since the way you improve your public speaking skills is by participating and competing) and you're a part of the team the moment you show up to a meeting. You get out of debate what you put in, and we only ask that you are a respectful and civil human being to others on the team!
